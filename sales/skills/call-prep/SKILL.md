# Federal Call / Meeting Prep — Aleut Federal

> Aleut Federal company context lives in [ALEUT-FEDERAL-CONTEXT.md](../../../ALEUT-FEDERAL-CONTEXT.md).

Prepare for any federal-facing meeting. This skill identifies the meeting type and tailors the prep accordingly — federal BD calls have very different rules and rhythms than commercial sales calls.

## Meeting Types We Prep For

1. **Sources sought / RFI response meeting** — pre-solicitation engagement with the agency.
2. **Industry day / pre-proposal conference** — formal market-research event.
3. **Capabilities briefing to an agency office** — discretionary, agency-permitted; usually small-business advocate or program office.
4. **Teaming / JV negotiation with a prime or sub** — TA, NDA, workshare, prime/sub structure.
5. **Mentor-Protégé meeting** — with a current or prospective SBA-approved mentor or protégé.
6. **CO / COR sync on an active contract** — coordinate with the operations and legal teams; many of these are CDRL or REA-driven and inappropriate for BD framing.
7. **Pre-award debrief** — after a competitive award decision (or non-selection); we can request a debrief; Procurement Integrity Act applies.
8. **Post-award kickoff** — internal + customer post-award meeting; clarify roles, CDRLs, milestones, billing.
9. **Conference / show meeting** — AFCEA, SAME, NCMA, AUSA, Sea-Air-Space, AFA, GovTech, Aleut shareholder events.

Ask which one if it's not obvious.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Identify the Meeting

Confirm meeting type, attendees, date, location/virtual, opportunity number(s), agency, and what we want out of it.

### Step 2: Apply the Right Rules

| Meeting type | Bright-line rules |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Sources sought / RFI | Treat as market research; provide capabilities aligned to the agency's stated needs; do not solicit non-public solicitation information; do not promote individual products; track contributions for later reference |
| Industry day | Listen-heavy; ask questions through the posted Q&A channel where required; PIA — never request or accept non-public information; no one-on-ones unless agency-organized |
| Capabilities briefing | Small-business office (OSDBU) is the standard channel for ANC/8(a) intros; do NOT lobby for a sole-source — provide capability, past performance, customer testimonials (non-endorsing), and let the CO/SBA evaluate eligibility |
| Teaming / JV | TA/NDA already executed; workshare aligned with FAR 52.219-14 (services 50% / general construction 15% / specialty 25% for the prime); 8(a) JV rules per 13 C.F.R. § 124.513; SBA approval for sole-source JVs |
| Mentor-Protégé | Discuss only within scope of the SBA-approved MPA; coordinate with legal on any expansion |
| CO / COR sync on active contract | Coordinate with PM + Legal; nothing changes scope/price/term except by written mod from the CO; document all directions |
| Debrief | Confidential; PIA-sensitive; we can take notes but cannot record without permission; questions must be focused on improving our future proposals; no protest-baiting questions |
| Post-award kickoff | Confirm key clauses, CDRLs, kickoff deliverables, billing, security/IT onboarding; route any clause questions to legal/contracts |
| Conference | Track who we met and what we learned; PIA still applies; gift rules apply if any federal employee attends |

### Step 3: Pull the Pre-Read

For each meeting type, assemble the pre-read:

- **Opportunity file** — solicitation, capture plan, win themes, draft pricing approach, teaming structure.
- **Customer file** — agency mission, recent strategy / OIG / GAO findings, recent awards in the space, CPARS where available.
- **Attendee file** — names, titles, backgrounds (from LinkedIn / agency press releases). **Apply PIA / 18 U.S.C. § 207 screen** if any federal employee on the agenda is being considered for future hire.
- **Past-performance pulls** — three to five recent, relevant references with customer, contract #, value, period of performance, scope match, CPARS rating where public.
- **Our differentiators** — ANC / 8(a) status, sole-source eligibility, Mentor-Protégé arrangement, Alaska-Native shareholder benefit, OCONUS capability, specific certifications (CMMC level, security clearances, ISO).
- **Risks and questions** — top 3 risks to surface; top 3 questions to ask.

### Step 4: Draft the Agenda

For each meeting type, draft an appropriate agenda. Default cadences:

- **Sources sought / industry day**: introductions → agency's needs → our relevant capability → past performance → questions (posted channel only).
- **Capabilities briefing**: introductions → ANC/8(a) overview → relevant past performance → differentiators → teaming/vehicle availability → questions.
- **Teaming negotiation**: TA status → workshare → roles/responsibilities → pricing roll-up approach → IP/data rights → exclusivity terms → next steps.
- **Debrief**: ground rules → ratings on each evaluation factor → our weaknesses/deficiencies → strengths → questions to clarify proposed approach gaps → close.
- **Post-award kickoff**: introductions → contract scope summary → key clauses → CDRLs/deliverables and dates → security/IT onboarding → billing/invoicing setup → comms plan → first 90-day milestones.

### Step 5: Confirm Talking Points and Bright Lines

- Win themes (3–4) we'll thread into the conversation.
- Differentiators tied to ANC / 8(a) only where appropriate to the meeting type.
- **Bright lines we won't cross** — no source-selection info exchange; no commitments outside CO authority; no inappropriate gifts (5 C.F.R. Part 2635); no implied endorsements; no overpromising on FAR 52.219-14 self-performance.

## Hard Rules

- **No source-selection-sensitive information.** Refuse it if offered.
- **No commitments outside CO authority** on active contracts.
- **No gifts to federal employees** beyond the de minimis rules (5 C.F.R. § 2635.204); coordinate any meal or token with Ethics.
- **No recording** of agency calls without explicit permission.
- **No solicitation of non-public information** during debriefs; questions must focus on improving our future proposals.
- **PIA / § 207 screen** if any federal attendee may move to us later.
- **Mandatory disclosure** — if a meeting surfaces credible FCA / criminal / significant overpayment evidence, route to GC under FAR 52.203-13.
