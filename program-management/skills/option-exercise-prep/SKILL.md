# /option-exercise-prep — Aleut Federal Program Management

> Aleut Federal company context lives in [ALEUT-FEDERAL-CONTEXT.md](../../../ALEUT-FEDERAL-CONTEXT.md).

Prepare for the customer's exercise of an option year (or option period). Done well, the option exercises smoothly and AF's revenue rolls forward; done poorly, the customer competes the work instead.

## Aleut Federal Context — Why Options Matter

Federal contracts often structure as a **base period + option periods** (often 4 options of 12 months each, giving a 5-year potential PoP). Option exercise is the **Government's unilateral right** under FAR 52.217-9 (Option to Extend the Term of the Contract).

Critical facts:

- **Government decision, on Government schedule** — typically 30–60 days before option period start.
- **No automatic** — the CO can choose not to exercise.
- **No negotiation right** for the contractor on whether to exercise.
- **Pricing** for the option period was typically locked in at award or via FAR 52.222-43 / SCA price adjustment mechanisms.
- **CPARS** performance during the prior period is a major factor in the CO's decision.
- **Re-compete vs. option** — if the CO declines an option, the work typically goes back to competition (recompete), which we may or may not win.

Aleut Federal's posture in the months before an option exercise determines whether the option is exercised. This skill is the structured prep.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Calendar the Option Decision Window

Per FAR 52.217-9, the CO must give notice within the time specified in the contract. Track:

- **Option period start date** (e.g., FY26 PoP starts Oct 1).
- **Notice deadline** — 30/60/90 days prior per the contract.
- **AF target date to confirm option-exercise direction** — 30 days before the notice deadline so we can adjust if needed.

### Step 2: Pre-Option Health Assessment (90 Days Out)

Full program self-assessment:

| Area | Assessment |
|------|-----------|
| **CPARS** | Interim CPARS narrative status; trends in Quality, Schedule, Cost Control, Management, SB Utilization, Regulatory Compliance |
| **Cost performance** | CPI trend (EVMS), burndown (FFP), funded vs billed (cost-type) |
| **Schedule performance** | SPI trend, milestones met, recovery plans |
| **Technical performance** | TPMs met; customer concerns; design-review pass |
| **Quality** | NCR rate; customer complaints; CPARS Quality feed |
| **Safety** | TRIR / DART trend; recordables; EM 385-1-1 findings |
| **Cyber / CMMC** | DFARS 252.204-7012 incidents; CMMC posture |
| **Sub / JV performance** | Workshare; flow-down compliance |
| **Staffing** | Key personnel continuity; clearance pipeline; FAR 52.219-14 evidence on set-asides |
| **Customer relationship** | CO / COR engagement health; open issues; perceived value-add |

Identify any **deal-breakers** — areas where customer dissatisfaction could lead the CO to decline the option. Address aggressively in the 60–90 days before the decision.

### Step 3: Pricing Update

- **SCA / Davis-Bacon Wage Determination refresh** — if the WD has moved since the prior period and the contract has FAR 52.222-43 / similar adjustment mechanism, prepare the equitable adjustment package.
- **EO 14026 minimum wage** — current rate confirmed; coverage applied where applicable.
- **Indirect rate forecast** — current actuals vs the rates embedded in the option-year pricing; if material variance, consider a FAR 42.704 rate adjustment.
- **Cost-of-living / labor-category** adjustments per contract terms.

### Step 4: Staffing Plan for the Option Period

- **Retention** of key personnel through the transition.
- **Clearance posture** — anyone losing clearance? In-process clearances expected to finalize?
- **CMMC posture** maintained for CUI work.
- **Sub / JV** continuity — do their staff continue?
- **FAR 52.219-14 self-performance** evidence for the next period if set-aside.

### Step 5: Customer Engagement

The COR's quiet read on the program directly affects the CO's option decision. In the 90-day window:

- **Walk through the prior-period CPARS narrative** with the COR — accurate? Anything to flag? Anything we can address before final CPARS?
- **Surface and resolve open issues** — close out NCRs, open action items, CDRL backlogs.
- **Demonstrate forward planning** — staffing, technical roadmap, risk treatment.
- **Be honest about anything Yellow / Red** — and present the recovery plan.

Don't lobby the CO directly on the option decision (it's their decision); do demonstrate AF is a continuing-value partner.

### Step 6: Confirm Option Exercise

Customer signs an SF 30 modification exercising the option. On receipt:

- **Distribute internally** — Program, Contracts, Finance, HR, Subs.
- **Update internal systems** — extended PoP, updated charge codes, refreshed CDRLs, updated WDs in payroll.
- **Communicate to subs / JV** — option exercised; their work continues; any flow-down changes.
- **Update Aleut Federal backlog** — funded backlog now includes the new option-year value.
- **CPARS final** for the prior period closes; new period CPARS interim begins.

### Step 7: If Option NOT Exercised

If the CO declines the option:

- **Don't dispute the decision** — it's the Government's right.
- **Request a debrief** — voluntary on FAR Subpart 16 / 17 actions but often granted; gives lessons for the recompete.
- **Pivot to recompete** — coordinate with BD / Capture (`sales/pipeline-review`, `sales/account-research`).
- **Plan transition** — phase-down, knowledge-transfer (often a contracted activity in the option period), property return, sub demob.
- **Aleut Corp / leadership** — material backlog loss is reportable.

## Hard Rules

- **No lobbying** for option exercise — CPARS, technical performance, and relationship are the levers.
- **Honest CPARS narrative discussions** — don't push back on legitimate findings.
- **Pricing adjustments per the contract** — SCA / WD / FAR 42.704 are formal mechanisms, not optional.
- **No commitments for the option period** until the CO has formally exercised — even strong COR signals aren't a commitment.
- **Mandatory disclosure (FAR 52.203-13)** — if the prep surfaces credible-evidence concerns, route to GC outside the option process.
