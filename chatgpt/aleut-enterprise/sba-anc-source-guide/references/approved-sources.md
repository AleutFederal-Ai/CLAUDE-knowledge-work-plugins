# Approved Sources for SBA / ANC Answers

Use this reference to decide whether a source is allowed.

## Official and controlling sources

Use these sources for legal, regulatory, compliance, and program answers:

- SBA.gov official pages, program pages, notices, FAQs, procedural notices, and press releases.
- eCFR / CFR, especially Title 13 for SBA rules.
- Federal Register proposed rules, final rules, notices, and requests for comment.
- U.S. Code via Congress.gov or official government sources.
- Acquisition.gov, including FAR and official acquisition regulations.
- SAM.gov for registration, entity, solicitation, and official notice facts.
- GAO decisions, reports, and bid protest decisions.
- SBA Office of Inspector General reports.
- Official agency procurement, acquisition, or small business program pages when the question concerns that agency's implementation.
- Court opinions from official court sources when litigation is directly relevant.

## Secondary sources allowed for news context only

Secondary sources may be used only for recent news context, not as the controlling basis for legal or compliance conclusions.

Allowed mainstream or established sources include:

- Associated Press, Reuters, Bloomberg, Wall Street Journal, Washington Post, New York Times, Politico, Government Executive, Federal News Network, Defense News, FedScoop, Nextgov/FCW, GovExec, Roll Call, The Hill.
- Established legal or government-contracting publications when clearly used as commentary or reporting, such as National Law Review, JD Supra, PubKGroup, Set-Aside Alert, Bloomberg Law, Law360, and law-firm client alerts.

When using these sources:

- Label them as secondary reporting or commentary.
- Cross-check against official documents whenever possible.
- Do not use them to override official sources.

## Disallowed sources

Do not use:

- Personal blogs, anonymous blogs, SEO content farms, random SBA commentary sites, unsupported social-media posts, YouTube commentary, Reddit, forums, or vendor marketing pages.
- Any source the user has not approved when it is being used for a factual or compliance claim.
- Secondary commentary as the sole source for current legal requirements, eligibility rules, or compliance obligations.

## Citation expectations

Cite the strongest source for each claim:

- Use statutes/regulations for eligibility requirements, definitions, restrictions, and exceptions.
- Use Federal Register for new or proposed rule changes, effective dates, and agency explanations.
- Use SBA.gov for operational guidance, program descriptions, forms, and agency-facing guidance.
- Use GAO or court opinions for bid protest or litigation conclusions.
- Use mainstream news only to establish that something was reported or to provide background context.
