## Company Snapshot

- **Legal name:** Aleut Federal, LLC (and operating subsidiaries)
- **Parent:** The Aleut Corporation — an **Alaska Native Corporation (ANC)** established under the Alaska Native Claims Settlement Act (ANCSA), 43 U.S.C. § 1601 et seq.
- **Lines of business:**
  - **Federal services** — professional, technical, IT, logistics, facilities, and mission-support services to U.S. federal agencies.
  - **Federal construction** — vertical and horizontal construction, design-build, renovation, and infrastructure delivery for federal customers.
- **Primary customers:** U.S. federal agencies and prime contractors flowing federal work down to us. Examples include DoD (Army/USACE, Navy/NAVFAC, Air Force/AFCEC, DLA), DHS, DOE, DOI, GSA, VA, State, USDA, NASA, and intelligence community customers.
- **Geographic footprint:** CONUS and OCONUS, with material work in Alaska, the Pacific (Hawaii, Guam, Pacific Islands), and the continental United States.

## Special Federal Status — ANC / 8(a)

Aleut Federal entities participate in the **SBA 8(a) Business Development Program** (13 C.F.R. Part 124) under the special rules that apply to Alaska Native Corporations and their subsidiaries. This drives most of our capture strategy. Key facts every skill should know:

- **Sole-source authority:** ANC-owned 8(a) firms can receive **sole-source 8(a) contracts of unlimited dollar value for civilian agencies** and up to the DoD sole-source threshold for defense work (currently $100M), pursuant to FAR 19.808-1 and 13 C.F.R. § 124.506(b). This is one of our most important market advantages.
- **No competition required** for qualifying sole-source 8(a) awards — but justifications must be properly documented (D&Fs, J&As where applicable).
- **Affiliation rules:** ANC subsidiaries are generally **not affiliated** with each other or with the parent for size purposes (13 C.F.R. § 121.103(b)(2)), which lets the family hold multiple 8(a) firms simultaneously and graduate them on independent schedules.
- **Tribal benefit obligation:** Under ANCSA Section 7(i)/(j) and SBA rules, profits are shared with Aleut shareholders. Skills that touch on community impact, ESG, or proposal narratives should reflect this — it is a genuine differentiator, not marketing.
- **Mentor-Protégé eligibility:** Active SBA Mentor-Protégé Program (13 C.F.R. § 125.9) relationships are used to expand past-performance and bonding capacity. Watch for joint-venture (JV) structures in pipeline and contracts.
- **Limitations on subcontracting:** FAR 52.219-14 — for services, prime must perform at least **50% of cost of personnel** with its own employees; for general construction, **15%** self-performance; for specialty trade construction, **25%**. Joint ventures count the JV partners' work toward the prime's share under 13 C.F.R. § 125.8(d). **Compliance with this clause is monitored at the contract level — skills dealing with subcontracting, capacity planning, or staffing should treat it as a hard constraint.**

## Regulatory Environment

Every plugin should default to the federal regulatory framework, not generic commercial assumptions.

### Acquisition regulations
- **Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR)** — 48 C.F.R. Chapter 1. Default rulebook for all federal contracts.
- **Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement (DFARS)** — 48 C.F.R. Chapter 2. Applies to all DoD work.
- **Agency supplements:** DEAR (DOE), AFARS (Army), NMCARS (Navy), AFFARS (Air Force), HSAR (DHS), GSAM (GSA), VAAR (VA), NFS (NASA), DOSAR (State), etc.
- **8(a) Program rules:** 13 C.F.R. Part 124; FAR Subpart 19.8.

### Labor & wage law (services and construction)
- **Service Contract Act (SCA) / Service Contract Labor Standards** — 41 U.S.C. ch. 67; FAR 52.222-41 and DOL Wage Determinations on **sam.gov**. Applies to most service contracts > $2,500. Skills dealing with comp, offers, or pricing on services contracts must default to the applicable WD.
- **Davis-Bacon Act (DBA) / Construction Wage Rate Requirements** — 40 U.S.C. ch. 31, subch. IV; FAR 52.222-6. Applies to federal construction contracts > $2,000. Prevailing wage and fringe benefit rules.
- **Contract Work Hours and Safety Standards Act (CWHSSA)** — overtime for laborers and mechanics on federal contracts > $100K.
- **Copeland Anti-Kickback Act** — payroll certification rules on construction (29 C.F.R. Part 3).
- **E-Verify** — FAR 52.222-54. Required for most federal contractors and applies to existing and new hires on covered contracts.
- **EEO obligations** — Executive Order 11246, OFCCP rules, VEVRAA (veterans), Section 503 (individuals with disabilities). Affirmative Action Plans required for many contracts.
- **EO 14026 minimum wage** for federal contractors (rate updated annually by DOL).
- **Drug-Free Workplace Act** — FAR 52.223-6.

### Construction-specific
- **Miller Act** — 40 U.S.C. §§ 3131-3134. Performance and payment bonds required on federal construction > $150K.
- **USACE EM 385-1-1** — Safety and Health Requirements Manual; standard on Corps of Engineers and many other federal construction sites.
- **OSHA 29 C.F.R. Part 1926** — construction safety standards.
- **NEPA, Clean Water Act, Clean Air Act, ESA, NHPA** — environmental and historic preservation compliance during construction.
- **Buy American Act / BABA (Build America, Buy America)** — domestic preference for materials on infrastructure projects (Public Law 117-58, Title IX).
- **Trade Agreements Act** — applies on covered contracts above the TAA threshold.

### Cost accounting & audit (services and construction)
- **FAR Part 31 — Cost Principles.** The default test for what costs are allowable, allocable, and reasonable on cost-reimbursable and T&M work.
- **Cost Accounting Standards (CAS)** — 48 C.F.R. Chapter 99. Modified or full CAS coverage triggers above thresholds; ANC 8(a) sole-source awards count.
- **DCAA** (Defense Contract Audit Agency) — performs incurred-cost audits, forward-pricing audits, accounting-system reviews, and floor checks. Our accounting system must be **DCAA-adequate** (SF 1408 criteria) to be eligible for cost-type work.
- **Indirect rates:** fringe, overhead, G&A, and (where applicable) material handling pools. Provisional billing rates are submitted annually; final rates are settled through **Incurred Cost Submissions (ICS)** within 6 months of fiscal year-end (FAR 52.216-7).
- **Truthful Cost or Pricing Data (TINA)** — formerly "Truth in Negotiations"; certified cost or pricing data required above the threshold (currently $2M).
- **Defective pricing** exposure if certified data is later found inaccurate, incomplete, or non-current.

### Cybersecurity & data
- **NIST SP 800-171** — protections for Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) on non-federal systems (DFARS 252.204-7012).
- **CMMC 2.0** — Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification. DoD contractors handling CUI need Level 2 (NIST 800-171 aligned). Rollout phased through 2025–2028; new DoD solicitations will require CMMC self-assessments or 3rd-party certification.
- **FedRAMP** — for cloud services provided to the government.
- **NIST SP 800-53** — control catalog for federal information systems.
- **ITAR / EAR** — export control where applicable (typically defense engineering work).
- **Section 889** — prohibition on covered telecommunications and video equipment (Huawei, ZTE, Hytera, Hikvision, Dahua). Annual representations and flow-downs required.

### Reporting, ethics, and integrity
- **SAM.gov** — System for Award Management. Annual registration and representations and certifications (FAR 52.204-8).
- **FAPIIS** — Federal Awardee Performance and Integrity Information System. Mandatory disclosures of certain criminal, civil, and administrative proceedings.
- **CPARS** — Contractor Performance Assessment Reporting System. Past performance ratings drive future awards.
- **FAR Subpart 3.10** — Contractor Code of Business Ethics and Conduct (FAR 52.203-13). Required ethics program, internal reporting, and **mandatory disclosure rule** (FAR 52.203-13(b)(3)(i)) for credible evidence of FCA violations, certain criminal violations, or significant overpayments.
- **False Claims Act (FCA) — 31 U.S.C. §§ 3729-3733** and **Anti-Kickback Act** exposure on virtually all federal payment requests.
- **Procurement Integrity Act** — 41 U.S.C. § 2101 et seq. Restrictions on hiring former federal officials and disclosure of source-selection / contractor bid information.
- **Lobbying restrictions** — FAR 52.203-12, 31 U.S.C. § 1352 (Byrd Amendment); SF-LLL filings.

## Hard Constraints / Do-Not-Dos

Skills should treat the following as bright lines:

1. **No legal advice.** Skills may help analyze, summarize, and draft, but the user must understand outputs are not legal opinions. For FAR/DFARS, FCA, or ethics questions, always route to qualified counsel and, where required, the **mandatory disclosure** path under FAR 52.203-13.
2. **No discussion of source-selection-sensitive or proprietary competitor information** outside legitimate channels — Procurement Integrity Act exposure.
3. **No commitments on behalf of the Contracting Officer.** Only the CO can change contract scope, price, period of performance, or terms. Skills that draft customer-facing communications should never imply otherwise.
4. **No promises of award.** Sole-source 8(a) eligibility is a tool, not a guarantee; SBA acceptance and CO discretion still apply.
5. **Protect CUI and ITAR data.** Skills should refuse to process material marked CUI, ITAR, EAR, or classified unless the environment is explicitly authorized for it.
6. **Section 889 hygiene.** Don't suggest covered telecom equipment or services from prohibited entities in any procurement output.
7. **Buy American / TAA hygiene.** When suggesting materials, suppliers, or COTS products on covered contracts, default to compliant country-of-origin.
