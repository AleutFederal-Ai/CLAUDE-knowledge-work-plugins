# /safety-stand-down — Aleut Federal Safety

> Aleut Federal company context lives in [ALEUT-FEDERAL-CONTEXT.md](../../../ALEUT-FEDERAL-CONTEXT.md).

Plan and execute a safety stand-down — a deliberate, full-stop pause that brings every worker on a site together to refocus on safety.

## Aleut Federal Context — When to Stand Down

Common triggers:

- **After a recordable injury** that suggests a systemic gap (not just a one-off).
- **After a fatality, hospitalization, or amputation** — always.
- **After a pattern of near-misses** on the same hazard.
- **Before a high-hazard phase** (e.g., starting major lift operations, vertical work at height, demolition).
- **OSHA / customer directive** — a stand-down may be required by a customer safety officer.
- **Industry-wide observances** — the OSHA-NIOSH-CPWR National Fall Prevention Stand-Down each spring; Construction Safety Week.
- **Climatic / seasonal** — heat illness ahead of summer; cold-weather work ahead of winter; weather event response.

A stand-down is **not** a substitute for incident investigation, AHA updates, or corrective actions. It's a communication mechanism on top of those.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Define the Trigger and Scope

- **Trigger** — the specific reason. Be honest: "two near-miss falls in the last 10 days" is more credible than "general safety refresh."
- **Scope** — which sites, which crews, which subs. A single project; multiple projects; the whole company.
- **Duration** — typical: 30 minutes to 2 hours; severe trigger may justify a half day or full day.
- **Timing** — first thing in the morning is most effective; mid-shift breaks momentum.

### Step 2: Plan the Content

A productive stand-down has 4 ingredients:

1. **Why we're doing this** — the specific trigger, briefed honestly without blaming individuals.
2. **What the hazard or topic is** — the specific safety issue (e.g., "fall protection on leading edges over 6 ft").
3. **What we're going to do differently** — concrete control changes (AHA update, equipment change, supervision change). NOT vague exhortations to "be more careful."
4. **What workers can do** — including how to raise a safety concern, how to invoke stop-work authority, whistleblower protections (29 U.S.C. § 660(c); FAR 52.203-17).

### Step 3: Notify the Customer

For federal contracts, notify the customer safety office in advance:

- Trigger and scope.
- Expected duration.
- Production-schedule impact (often zero — productivity rises after a good stand-down).
- Whether customer representatives should attend (USACE district safety officers often appreciate the invite).

### Step 4: Notify Subcontractors

All subs on the affected sites participate. Provide the topic and time at least 48 hours ahead unless the trigger is acute.

### Step 5: Execute

- All work stops.
- Personnel gather (in-person preferred; video-conference for OCONUS or multi-site).
- Brief content in the order above.
- Allow workers to speak — listen for concerns we haven't surfaced.
- Document attendance (sign-in roster).
- Sign-in roster is **also** evidence of training, which matters for the OSHA / EM 385-1-1 file.

### Step 6: Tangible Output

A stand-down without follow-through is a check-the-box exercise. Output should include:

- **Updated AHA(s)** for the affected activity.
- **Updated APP** if the topic affects company-level approach.
- **Specific equipment / control changes** with owners and dates.
- **Training scheduled** for any qualification gaps surfaced.
- **Customer briefing** on what we changed.

### Step 7: Communicate Across AF

A stand-down at one site often surfaces lessons relevant to other AF projects. Share:

- Summary of trigger and findings.
- Specific changes.
- Recommend or require the same changes at sister sites.

## Hard Rules

- **Don't blame individuals.** Stand-downs are about system-level fixes; personal accountability is a separate conversation.
- **Don't skip the customer notification** on federal-customer sites.
- **Don't omit subs.** They're on our site; the stand-down applies to them.
- **Document attendance.** It's training evidence; it's defense in audit.
- **Don't bill the stand-down to the contract as productive work.** Coordinate with Contracts on how the time is captured — typically as a non-productive but allowable cost on cost-type contracts.
- **Follow through with tangible changes** within 30 days. A stand-down with no follow-through erodes credibility.
