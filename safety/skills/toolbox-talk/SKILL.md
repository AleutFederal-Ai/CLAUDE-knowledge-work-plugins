# /toolbox-talk — Aleut Federal Safety

> Aleut Federal company context lives in [ALEUT-FEDERAL-CONTEXT.md](../../../ALEUT-FEDERAL-CONTEXT.md).

Daily toolbox safety talk — the short, focused, every-morning briefing that anchors a safe site.

## Aleut Federal Context — Why a Daily Toolbox Matters

- **EM 385-1-1** expects daily safety briefings.
- **AHAs are briefed daily** before the activity starts — the toolbox talk is the briefing venue.
- **OSHA 1926.21(b)(2)** instruction requirement is partly satisfied by daily talks.
- **Subcontractors** participate — federal customers expect prime to coordinate sub safety daily.
- **Davis-Bacon workers** — toolbox time is compensable; track in timekeeping correctly.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Plan (the day before, or first 15 minutes of the morning)

- **What activities are running today?** — Look at the work plan.
- **Which AHA(s) apply?** — Pull them.
- **What conditions have changed?** — Weather, deliveries, visitors, equipment status.
- **Any recent near-misses or incidents?** — Carry the lessons forward.
- **Any inspections expected today?** — OSHA / USACE / customer? Brief the team.
- **Any new workers or visitors on site?** — They need orientation (separate or as part of toolbox).
- **Any worker concerns from prior days?** — Close the loop.

### Step 2: Pick a Single Focus Topic

Even a 10-minute talk should have ONE primary topic. The brain holds about three new ideas; concentrate.

Sources for the topic:
- The highest-hazard activity of the day.
- A recent near-miss.
- A seasonal hazard (heat, cold, rain, wind).
- A regulatory anniversary (OSHA Fall Prevention Stand-Down, etc.).
- A worker-raised concern.

### Step 3: Deliver — 10 to 20 Minutes

Structure:

1. **Today's plan** (2 min) — what's happening, where, by whom.
2. **AHA review** for the high-hazard activity (3-5 min) — principal steps + their controls.
3. **The focus topic** (5-10 min) — concrete, with a story or a recent example.
4. **Conditions** (2 min) — weather, site changes, deliveries.
5. **Worker concerns** (3-5 min) — invite, listen, commit to follow-through.
6. **Stop-work reminder** — every worker has stop-work authority; how to invoke; FAR 52.203-17 whistleblower protections.

Avoid:
- Reading off a generic toolbox-talk PDF.
- Vague language ("be careful out there").
- Skipping the worker-concerns portion.
- Running past 20 minutes — people stop listening.

### Step 4: Document Attendance

Sign-in sheet captures:
- Date and time.
- Site / project.
- AHA(s) briefed.
- Focus topic.
- Briefer (typically SSHO or competent person).
- Each attendee's printed name and signature.
- Sub attendees by name and company.

File in the project safety binder — needed for OSHA / USACE / customer audit.

### Step 5: Follow Through on Concerns

Anything raised gets a written response and an owner. Closing the loop is the difference between a toolbox program workers respect and one they tune out.

## Toolbox Topic Inventory (rotating monthly)

Examples to rotate:

- Fall protection at heights / leading edges.
- Excavation and trenching (Subpart P).
- Confined space entry.
- Hot work / fire watch.
- Crane safety / outrigger setup.
- Material handling / ergonomic.
- Heat illness prevention.
- Cold-weather work.
- Electrical safety / arc flash.
- PPE — selection, inspection, replacement.
- Hazard communication / SDS access.
- Hand and power tool safety.
- Housekeeping and slip / trip / fall.
- Driving safety (especially OCONUS / remote routes).
- Active shooter / workplace violence (where applicable).
- Bloodborne pathogens / first aid response.
- Severe weather response.
- Vehicle / equipment backing.
- Lockout / tagout.
- Suspended loads / load paths.
- Working alone (especially remote OCONUS sites).
- Substance use / fitness for duty.

## Hard Rules

- **Daily, not "as needed".** Even on quiet days, hold the talk.
- **AHA-driven, not generic.** Tie content to the day's actual work.
- **Workers speak.** A monologue is not a toolbox talk.
- **Document attendance.** Sign-in is evidence; missing it is a finding.
- **Close the loop** on worker concerns within 48 hours.
- **Stop-work authority** is reaffirmed in every talk; FAR 52.203-17 whistleblower protections at least once a month.
